General Recursion

PFPL chapters 19 and 20 | TAPL chapter 11.11

Key idea: Exchange totality for universality

Increase expressive power of programs at the expense of termination guarantees

Surveying the Language Landscape

Languages we've explored thus far:

Name Properties
System T
(PFPL ch. 9)		 natural numbers, higher-order functions, sum & product types, totality, type safety
System M
(PFPL ch. 15)		 System T plus (co-)inductive types, via partial recursion
System F
(PFPL ch. 16)		 System M plus parametric polymorphic types
System FE
(PFPL ch. 17)		 System F plus existentially-quantified types
System Fω
(PFPL ch. 18)		 System FE plus universally-quantified types

Something's Missing...

System Fω is powerful, but limited and cumbersome:

  • Total languages are not universal
  • Programs must encode their termination proofs

Enter PCF

Plotkin, 1977 | PFPL ch. 19

System Fω plus general recursion via partial functions and fixed-point operator

Programs remain well-typed, but may diverge (i.e., not terminate)

Lazy vs. eager dynamics become subtle because sub-computations may diverge
